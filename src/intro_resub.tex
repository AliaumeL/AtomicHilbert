\section{Introduction for resubmission}\label{sec:intro resub}
%
Let $\K$ be an arbitrary field.
The classical Hilbert's basis theorem says that every ideal in a polynomial ring over finitely many variables are coefficients from $\K$ is finitely generated.
A Gröbner basis is a specific kind of generators for polynomial ideals with good computational properties.
Given a Gröbner basis for an ideal $\idl$,
one can easily decide if another polynomial is in $\idl$ by multivariate polynomial division.
Gröbner bases of the sum $\idl[I] + \idl[J]$ and the intersection $\idl[I] \cap \idl[J]$ of two ideals $\idl[I]$ and $\idl[J]$ can be computed from the Gröbner bases of $\idl[I]$ and $\idl[J]$.
Given a finite set of generators of an ideal $\idl[I]$,
the Buchberger's algorithm can be used to compute a Gröbner basis for the ideal.

Gröbner bases are used for  ILP, Pol Eq
